AIML Course Syllabus
Duration: 15 weeks
Hours per week: 4 (Total: 60 hours)
Format: Lectures, hands-on labs, projects, and discussions

Learning Outcomes
By the end of this course, students will be able to:
- Apply Python and ML libraries to real datasets
- Build, train, and evaluate ML/DL models
- Understand ethical implications of AI systems
- Develop end-to-end AIML solutions for practical problems

Week 1: Introduction to AI & ML – history, applications, basics
Week 2: Python for AIML – NumPy, Pandas, Matplotlib
Week 3: Data Preprocessing – missing data, normalization, feature engineering
Week 4: Supervised Learning I – linear regression, logistic regression
Week 5: Supervised Learning II – decision trees, random forests, SVM
Week 6: Model Evaluation – accuracy, precision, recall, F1, ROC
Week 7: Neural Networks Basics – perceptron, activation functions, backpropagation
Week 8: Deep Learning I – CNNs for image recognition
Week 9: Deep Learning II – RNNs, LSTMs for sequence data
Week 10: Unsupervised Learning I – clustering (K-means, hierarchical)
Week 11: Unsupervised Learning II – dimensionality reduction (PCA, t-SNE)
Week 12: Reinforcement Learning – agents, environments, Q-learning
Week 13: AI Ethics & Fairness – bias, transparency, responsible AI
Week 14: AIML Applications – NLP, computer vision, recommender systems
Week 15: Capstone Project & Review – real-world AIML application, presentations
